Experience Diving.

Equipment Introduction

Experience diving generally uses scuba equipment, including a scuba tank, hoses, regulator, buoyancy control device (BCD), mask, and fins.

Characteristics

Experience diving is a program designed for non-professionals to try diving. It allows individuals to experience diving under the guidance of a professional diver in a safe environment.

Pros and Cons

Pros : Requires a short amount of time to experience diving and does not need professional skills or experience.
cons : Experience diving is usually conducted at shallow depths, and the diving time may be limited.

Free Diving.

Equipment Introduction

Freediving requires a mask, fins, snorkel, and a freediving wetsuit.

Characteristics

Freediving is an activity where you hold your breath and move freely underwater without the use of breathing equipment.

Pros and Cons

Pros : Freediving can improve mental relaxation and physical agility, and allows you to experience the beauty of nature.
cons : As it involves holding your breath, there are safety concerns, and learning freediving techniques takes time.

Scuba Diving.

Equipment Introduction

Scuba diving requires a scuba tank, regulator, buoyancy control device (BCD), mask, fins, boots, and a reusable mesh bag.

Characteristics

Scuba diving is an activity where you use breathing equipment to move underwater. It can be enjoyed in various environments such as different depths and underwater caves.

Pros and Cons

Pros : Scuba diving allows you to observe a variety of marine life, explore deep underwater terrain, and spend more time underwater.
cons : Scuba diving requires professional skills and equipment, and always needs management and training for safe diving.

Fun Diving.

Equipment Introduction

Fun diving primarily uses scuba equipment.

Characteristics

Fun diving is diving purely for enjoyment, aiming to enhance the diving experience and explore new areas.

Pros and Cons

Pros : Fun diving brings joy and excitement through diving and helps improve diving skills.
cons : Unlike professional scuba diving, fun diving may have less clear goals or training, and may require a more in-depth experience.
